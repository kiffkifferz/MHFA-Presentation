Acknowledging Biases & Limitations

For full transparency, it's important to consider the context of this data.

Small Sample Size

The survey reflects feedback from 13 out of 65 contacted past participants. While the insights are valuable, they may not represent the views of all attendees.

Relationship Bias

A majority of respondents had a pre-existing relationship with the instructor, which could correlate with a more positive and engaged experience.

Positivity Bias

Individuals who had a positive experience are generally more likely to respond to feedback surveys, potentially skewing the results in a favorable direction.

SPEAKER NOTES

"Before we move to recommendations, I want to address the limitations of this survey for full transparency. The sample size is small, and we recognize there's a potential for both relationship bias and positivity bias in the results. This doesn't invalidate the positive findings, but it provides important context that the results reflect a highly engaged, positive subset of participants."

Strategic Recommendations

The data points toward a clear path for program evolution and growth.

1. EXPAND CURRICULUM

Offer Advanced 'Deep Dive' Courses

Capitalize on participant interest by developing advanced modules on the most requested topics: Mental Health & Diet, Trauma, and ADHD.

2. OPTIMIZE DELIVERY

Standardize the Optimal Format

The data shows a clear preference for fully in-person delivery. Within this format, a schedule of two 4-hour sessions can cater to professional timetables while maximizing engagement.

3. REFINE PRICING STRATEGY

Implement a Two-Track Model

Introduce standard fees for corporate and professional clients, while using grants and sponsorships to fund a low-cost track for increased accessibility.

SPEAKER NOTES

"With that context in mind, we have three strategic recommendations. First, to Expand the curriculum by offering the advanced, 'deep-dive' courses that participants are asking for. Second, to Optimize delivery by standardizing the format that combines the most preferred elements: in-person delivery, broken into two 4-hour sessions. And third, to Refine the pricing strategy with a two-track model to ensure both financial sustainability and continued community access."
